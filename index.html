About Buffy Howard

Boards don't Google turnaround specialists. They pick up the phone and ask the one question that matters: Can we get Buffy? Every crisis. One answer. Buffy.

A former CEO turned consultant, Buffy Howard walks in and takes charge when organizations are on the brink of collapse and out of options. She fixes finances, operations, culture, and compliance simultaneously, from the inside, not the sidelines. When you need a full rescue and not a report, Buffy delivers transformation across every dimension by starting where others won't: the people. Her philosophy is simple: when employees thrive, every other metric follows. Buffy Howard is the specialist organizations call when nothing else has worked. The one who walks into a crisis and doesn't leave until the organization is functioning, financially sound, and built to last.

The person behind the work

Fast. Serious.
Genuinely human.

Buffy Howard holds a Ph.D. in Leadership Studies and an MBA, but her real education happened in the field — inside failing organizations, fractured teams, and regulatory crises where the stakes were people's livelihoods and patients' care. She has also been on the other side of the table: as the owner and operator of a successful Adult Family Home in Washington State, she knows what it feels like to sign the checks, manage the staff, and be accountable for everything.

She has been described as fast, serious, down to earth, and fun. She brings an energy into a room that makes people believe things can actually get better — and then she proves it. She is not a report writer. She is not a slideshow consultant. She walks in, takes charge, and stays until the job is done.

Based in Tacoma, Washington, Buffy works with a small number of organizations at a time, going all in on each one until the crisis is resolved and the foundation is solid. Outside of work she is a competitive powerlifter, an avid traveler across 15 countries, and speaks conversational Khmer (Cambodian).

The origin story

It started in junior high school — a teenager volunteering at a nursing home in Bethlehem, PA, drawn to the work before she even understood why. Her first job out of college was at a nursing home in Washington State, this one in the middle of a regulatory crisis. They called it the War Room — giant Post-it notes covering every wall, citations everywhere, everyone scrambling. Buffy walked in, looked around, and felt something click. This was exactly the kind of work she was meant to do.

She was right. She got her Nursing Home Administrator license, took over her first facility — poorly run, losing money, unhappy staff, poor care — and turned it around. Then she did it again. And again. For fifteen years at the same company, she was the person they moved from facility to facility once the work was done. Fix one, move to the next.

That pattern followed her everywhere she went. As a Regional Vice President at a national urgent care and workers' compensation company — her first move outside of long-term care — she inherited a failing multi-state region and turned it around, exceeding EBITDA targets by $14 million in a single year. At a five-clinic radiation oncology practice in Washington State, she stepped into a fragmented organization and built the infrastructure it had never had — centralized HR, compliance systems, and operational alignment across all five clinics — while improving culture, patient experience, and financial performance simultaneously. At a multi-site ophthalmology practice, she took the CEO seat, identified major revenue cycle failures within 30 days, and delivered a cost reduction of 25% and revenue growth of 10%.

Sprinkled in was a stint leading value-based care initiatives — not a turnaround, but an experience that gave her deep expertise in alternative payment models, Medicare strategy, and payer dynamics that is indispensable for any healthcare organization navigating today's reimbursement landscape.

The throughline across 25 years and every organization: she didn't go looking for broken things. Broken things found her. And she has never once left one the way she found it.
